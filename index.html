Currently, there is no precise consensus on the probability, and it is generally considered a low-probability scenario, though it cannot be entirely ruled out.Factors Influencing the Likelihood:Tensions Between Superpowers:Tensions between the United States, China, and Russia are a key factor. Issues such as trade, technology (AI, cyber warfare), and control over resources (e.g., energy and minerals) increase friction.Regional conflicts (e.g., Taiwan, Ukraine, or the Middle East) could escalate to involve major powers.Nuclear Weapons and Deterrence:The existence of nuclear weapons acts as a deterrent to all-out war, as the consequences would be catastrophic for all parties (Mutual Assured Destruction - MAD).However, miscalculations or accidents could lead to unintended escalation.Technology and Cyber Warfare:Cyberattacks on critical infrastructure (e.g., power grids, banks, military systems) could provoke severe responses.Advances in AI and autonomous weapons increase the risk of "unplanned" wars due to automation.Economic and Social Factors:Economic crises, climate change, and resource shortages (e.g., food, water) could heighten instability and fuel conflicts.Populist movements and political polarization within nations could weaken the ability to prevent escalation.International Cooperation:Institutions like the UN, NATO, and international agreements (e.g., arms control treaties) reduce the risk by promoting dialogue.However, the weakening of these institutions or lack of trust between nations could increase the risk.Probability Estimates:Short Term (5-10 years): The likelihood is relatively low (expert estimates range from 5%-15% for extreme scenarios), as major powers are aware of the devastating consequences of a global war.Long Term (20-50 years): The likelihood could increase if solutions are not found for crises like climate change, resource scarcity, or the proliferation of lethal weapons. However, accurate long-term predictions are challenging.Possible Scenarios:Large-Scale Conventional War: A regional conflict (e.g., China-Taiwan) escalates to involve major powers but remains limited (without nuclear weapons).Cyber or Hybrid Warfare: Cyberattacks, propaganda, and sabotage cause significant damage without a full-scale physical war.Nuclear War: The most catastrophic scenario, but also the least likely due to mutual deterrence.How Can the Risk Be Reduced?Strengthening diplomacy and international cooperation.Establishing agreements to control weapons and dangerous technologies (e.g., military AI).Addressing global crises like climate change and economic inequality.Improving communication between superpowers to prevent misunderstandings.Summary:The likelihood of a Third World War depends on the decisions of leaders, technological developments, and humanity’s ability to manage crises. What We Do?

We believe that we can save more lifes with you

Healthy Food

No child should go to bed hungry. In conflict zones, access to healthy food becomes a luxury. Children are surviving on scraps, leading to malnutrition, stunted growth, and weakened immune systems. Your donation helps deliver nutritious meals packed with vitamins and energy to fuel growing bodies and restore health. Give the gift of strength through food.

Pure Water

Clean water is a right, not a privilege. When war destroys infrastructure, clean water becomes dangerously scarce. Contaminated water sources cause deadly diseases like cholera and typhoid—especially in children. With your help, we can build safe water systems, distribute filtration kits, and provide emergency bottled water to families in desperate need. One drop can save a life.

Health Care

Wounds can heal. So can trauma—if help arrives in time. In warzones, medical care is either overwhelmed or wiped out entirely. Children are left without treatment for injuries, infections, or emotional trauma. Your support funds mobile clinics, emergency surgeries, vaccines, and mental health care to restore hope and health. Because every child deserves a chance to heal.

Primary Education

War can take away homes—but it shouldn't take away futures. Education offers more than knowledge—it brings stability, normalcy, and the power to dream beyond war. Millions of children have lost access to school. Your donation helps build temporary learning centers, supply books and uniforms, and fund trained volunteer teachers. Empower a child today to rebuild their tomorrow.

Residence Facilities

Shelter means safety. Shelter means survival. Displaced families are living in ruins, tents, or exposed to the harsh weather. With nowhere to sleep, children face illness, danger, and fear. We are working to provide safe, dignified shelters where families can sleep, eat, and feel human again. Your donation builds more than walls—it builds protection.

Social Care

Compassion is a lifeline. Children affected by war need more than supplies—they need emotional care, guidance, and a sense of belonging. Through child protection services, trauma counseling, community support, and foster placement, we ensure they’re not just surviving—but cared for. With your help, we can bring back joy, trust, and healing.

In the shadow of World War III, where nations clash and borders crumble, there is a silent cry that too often goes unheard—the cry of the innocent. They are children. Some have watched their homes turn to rubble. Others have seen their parents vanish in a cloud of dust and gunfire. They don’t understand why their schools are now graveyards, why their playgrounds have become battlegrounds, or why hunger gnaws at them day and night. They are the forgotten faces of war. But they don't have to be. As the world reels under the impact of violence, displacement, and fear, these children need more than sympathy—they need action. They need food, clean water, shelter, and hope. They need someone to say, “We see you, and we will not let you suffer in silence.”
